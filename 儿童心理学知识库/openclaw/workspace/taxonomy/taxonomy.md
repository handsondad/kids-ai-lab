## Scenes

- `separation_reunion`: separating from or reconnecting with caregiver
- `bedtime`: before sleep, calming and settling
- `meal`: meal table or snack time interaction
- `playtime`: free play, floor play, pretend play, shared play
- `cleanup`: cleanup, stopping play, shifting away from preferred activity
- `transition`: leaving home, switching activity, moving between routines
- `public_outing`: store, restaurant, travel, crowded places
- `preschool_dropoff`: school or daycare handoff
- `medical_visit`: clinic, vaccination, checkup, unfamiliar procedure
- `sibling_interaction`: sibling tension, sharing, jealousy, repair
- `peer_interaction`: peers, turn-taking, conflict, joining play
- `frustration_moment`: blocked goal, failed attempt, strong frustration
- `tantrum_recovery`: after escalation, calming and reconnecting
- `boundary_setting`: saying no, holding line, enforcing limit
- `one_on_one`: one adult and one child
- `group`: siblings or multiple children
- `quiet_connection`: calm reconnection, cuddle, reading, low stimulation
- `toilet_routine`: potty training, cleanup, bathroom routine

## Domains

- `attachment_security`: security, trust, safe base, reunion patterns
- `co_regulation`: adult-supported calming and state regulation
- `emotional_regulation`: managing intensity, recovery, staying organized
- `emotion_recognition`: noticing and naming internal states and feelings
- `frustration_tolerance`: handling blocked goals, waiting, trying again
- `autonomy`: independence, initiative, self-direction, ownership
- `impulse_control`: pause, inhibit, shift, accept limit
- `boundary_internalization`: understanding and gradually accepting rules
- `separation_adjustment`: preparing for and recovering from separation
- `social_connection`: shared attention, reciprocity, felt connection
- `empathy`: noticing others' feelings and repairing impact
- `peer_skills`: joining play, turn-taking, conflict navigation
- `play_development`: symbolic play, shared play, flexible play themes
- `self_concept`: competence, identity, shame resilience, confidence
- `anxiety_fear`: fear response, caution, avoidance, reassurance needs
- `routine_adaptation`: coping with predictability, change, transitions
- `sensory_regulation`: managing sensory load, overstimulation, downshifting

## Child Signals

- `clinginess`: increased need for proximity or holding
- `separation_distress`: crying, panic, protest around separation
- `tantrum`: rapid escalation, screaming, collapsing, loss of control
- `hitting_or_biting`: aggressive body action under stress or impulse
- `refusal`: strong no, dropping out, resisting direction
- `bedtime_resistance`: delay, protest, repeated return, increased intensity at bedtime
- `toilet_resistance`: avoidance, fear, withholding, refusal around toilet routine
- `fearfulness`: strong caution, avoidance, freezing, reassurance seeking
- `aggression`: throwing, pushing, hitting, verbal attack
- `withdrawn_state`: shutting down, disengaging, unusually low participation
- `overstimulation`: hyperactivity, disorganization, easily triggered escalation
- `sibling_jealousy`: rivalry, protest, attention competition after sibling event
- `peer_conflict`: grabbing, exclusion, difficulty entering or sustaining play
- `control_struggle`: repeated bargaining, power struggle, control seeking

## Support Strategies

- `emotion_naming`: label feeling and likely cause in simple language
- `validation`: acknowledge the child's internal experience without endorsing behavior
- `co_regulation`: use calm presence, rhythm, proximity, breath, or pacing to help organize state
- `connection_before_direction`: reconnect before giving instruction or correction
- `routine_preview`: preview what will happen and what comes next
- `transition_warning`: warn ahead of change and anchor the next step
- `choice_with_boundaries`: offer limited choices inside a firm boundary
- `limit_with_empathy`: hold line while showing understanding
- `model_calm`: lower voice, pace, and body intensity to reduce escalation
- `play_reflection`: use play to mirror, process, or rehearse experience
- `scaffold_problem_solving`: break challenge into smaller steps and co-think solutions
- `repair_after_conflict`: reconnect and repair after rupture or limit-setting
- `reduce_stimulation`: lower input, simplify language, trim demands, reduce audience
- `predictable_routine`: stabilize sequence, cue, and expectation across repetitions
- `observe_and_track`: notice patterns, triggers, recovery time, and context before changing plan
- `special_time`: build short, consistent one-on-one connection time

## Materials

- `no_material`: no special material required
- `picture_books`: picture books or simple story books
- `comfort_object`: blanket, stuffed animal, familiar item
- `visual_schedule`: picture strip, sequence card, timer, first-then cue
- `pretend_play_toys`: dolls, figures, kitchen toys, vehicles, stuffed animals
- `art_materials`: paper, crayons, clay, stickers
- `sensory_materials`: water, playdough, kinetic sand, soft textures
- `household_items`: cups, towels, boxes, pillows, laundry basket
- `calm_corner_tools`: pillow, breathing card, sensory bottle, quiet basket
- `outdoor_space`: walk path, playground, open outdoor area

## Energy Levels

- `low`: calm, quiet, low movement
- `medium`: moderate movement or engagement
- `high`: active body movement, high excitement

## Difficulty

- `easy`: simple language and fast start
- `medium`: multi-step guidance needed
- `hard`: sustained co-regulation or stronger scaffolding needed

## Adult Effort

- `low`: minimal setup and guidance
- `medium`: some setup or active scaffolding needed
- `high`: continuous facilitation or larger preparation needed
