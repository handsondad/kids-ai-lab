# Taxonomy

Use the following controlled vocabulary whenever possible.
If a new tag is needed, add it here first before using it widely.

- `age_12_18m`: 12-18 months
- `age_18_24m`: 18-24 months
- `age_24_36m`: 24-36 months
- `age_3_4y`: 3-4 years
- `age_4_6y`: 4-6 years

## Scenes

- `feeding`: feeding or milk time
- `diapering`: changing diaper or cleaning up
- `bath`: bath routine
- `bedtime`: before sleep, calm state preferred
- `book_reading`: shared reading
- `playtime`: toys, floor play, free play
- `outdoor`: walk, park, neighborhood observation
- `meal`: meal table conversation
- `waiting`: queue, clinic, restaurant waiting
- `travel`: car, train, airplane
- `transition`: before bath, before leaving home, after cleanup
- `one_on_one`: one adult and one child
- `group`: siblings or multiple children
- `pretend_play`: symbolic or role play
- `emotional_moment`: sadness, frustration, excitement, recovery moment

## Domains

- `joint_attention`: shared attention, gaze following, pointing, showing
- `gesture_communication`: gestures, waving, pointing, body-based communication
- `receptive_language`: understanding words, phrases, simple directions
- `expressive_language`: using sounds, words, phrases, sentences
- `vocabulary`: naming and concept growth
- `sentence_building`: combining words into longer utterances
- `question_answering`: answering simple questions and asking questions
- `narrative`: retelling, sequencing, describing past events
- `social_pragmatics`: turn-taking, topic maintenance, conversational flexibility
- `emotion_language`: naming feelings and linking feelings with situations
- `speech_sound`: clarity of sounds and pronunciation development
- `early_literacy`: rhyme, print awareness, story language, listening to books

## Support Strategies

- `naming`: label what the child sees, does, or feels
- `imitation`: imitate the child's sounds, words, or play actions
- `pause_and_wait`: say less, pause, and leave a response slot
- `expansion`: add one step to what the child already said
- `recast`: keep the child's meaning and model a clearer form
- `parallel_talk`: describe what the child is doing in the moment
- `self_talk`: describe what the adult is doing in the moment
- `choices`: offer two meaningful verbal choices
- `open_question`: use prompts that invite longer responses
- `routine_embedding`: put language support into daily routines
- `visual_support`: use gesture, pointing, picture, or sequence cue
- `turn_taking`: structure short back-and-forth exchanges

## Materials

- `no_material`: no special material required
- `books`: picture books or reading material
- `household_items`: cups, spoons, towels, boxes, pillows
- `toys`: dolls, cars, blocks, pretend-play toys
- `picture_cards`: printed cards or photos
- `music`: songs, rhythm tools, speakers
- `outdoor_objects`: leaves, stones, vehicles, signs, animals seen outside

## Energy Levels

- `low`: calm, quiet, low movement
- `medium`: moderate movement or engagement
- `high`: active body movement, high excitement

## Difficulty

- `easy`: simple language and fast start
- `medium`: multi-step guidance needed
- `hard`: sustained attention or stronger scaffolding needed

## Adult Effort

- `low`: minimal setup and guidance
- `medium`: some setup or active scaffolding needed
- `high`: continuous facilitation or larger preparation needed
